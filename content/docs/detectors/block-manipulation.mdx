title: Block Manipulation
description: 3 detectors (BM-01…BM-03) for miner-influenceable block variables, transaction ordering, and weak randomness.

Block-manipulation detectors (`BM`) catch reliance on values a miner or an ordering
adversary can influence — timestamps, transaction order, and randomness derived from
block data. **3 detectors**.

| ID | Slug | Severity | Confidence | What it detects |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| BM-01 | `dangerous-block-timestamp` | Low | High | `block.timestamp` / `now` used in decision logic (conditions, returns, arguments) — miner-influenceable. |
| BM-02 | `transaction-order-dependency` | Medium | Low | Contract state depends on transaction ordering — front-running / race condition. |
| BM-03 | `weak-prng` | High | High | Randomness derived from block variables — a predictable, weak PRNG. |

<Callout title="Remediation">
  Don't use `block.timestamp` or block hashes as a source of randomness or as a
  precise clock — use a verifiable randomness source such as Chainlink VRF, and
  design around front-running (commit–reveal, slippage bounds).
</Callout>
